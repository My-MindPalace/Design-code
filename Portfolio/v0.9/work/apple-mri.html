Flipkart Shipped · 2024 Seller Experience Device Diagnostics

Flipkart Diagnostic
+ Apple MRI

Refurbished devices on Flipkart had a trust problem. Apple had the fix. The challenge was making it work for a store owner in Patna who's never heard of MRI.

Role Lead UX Designer
Platform Flipkart Seller App (iOS + Android)
Launched Q3 2024 · Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru

The problem

When a buyer lands on a refurbished iPhone listing, they're reading a paragraph someone typed. No standard. No verification.

Condition descriptions ranged from meticulous to wildly optimistic — and buyers knew it. Return rates were high, disputes were frequent, and the refurbished category had a credibility ceiling it couldn't break through. The business wanted to grow refurbished. Trust was the blocker.

What we built

Apple MRI is Apple's own hardware diagnostic — it runs a battery of tests on iPhones and iPads and returns a standardised condition report. Flipkart got access. Our job was to integrate it into the seller listing flow in a way that non-technical sellers and third-party surveyors could actually use.

We also brought in Yaantra as a parallel diagnostic for Android devices — building both tools into a unified flow so sellers have the same verified listing experience regardless of what device they're selling.

Two very different users, one flow

The Refurbished Seller

Small shop owner · 30–50 devices/month

Lists devices on Flipkart daily. Not technically trained. Doesn't know what IMEI stands for. Needs the whole diagnostic done — ideally while they're at the counter handling another sale.

"Just tell me what to press and when it's done."

The Surveyor

Certified field technician

Travels to sellers to run the actual diagnostic. Semi-technical. Juggles multiple appointments. Has seen every edge case — wrong device, wrong iOS version, dead battery, dual SIM confusion.

"I need to know exactly what to do when something breaks mid-diagnostic."

Design decisions

Six phases. Each one had its own set of problems.

01

Surveyor selection Seller

Sellers had to pick someone from a list of certified surveyors — but the list had no context. No availability, no distance, no rating. They were picking names blind, scheduling back and forth over WhatsApp, and diagnostic sessions were frequently falling through.

We surfaced availability windows, distance, and a rating directly on the selection card. Added a "nearest available" default that could be overridden. Selection time dropped from an average 4 minutes to under 60 seconds.

Tier 2 problem

Spotty connectivity meant the surveyor list sometimes wouldn't load. We pre-fetch and cache surveyor data so the screen works on a weak connection — no spinner, no dead end.

02

IMEI validation Surveyor

We discovered sellers were sometimes running diagnostics on a different device than the one listed. The report ended up attached to the wrong listing. Buyers were receiving devices that didn't match their verified report.

Camera scan at the start of every session. IMEI cross-referenced against the listing in real time. If it doesn't match, you can't proceed.

Manual fallback

Worn IMEI labels are common in second-hand devices. Manual entry reduced drop-off at this step by 18%.

Dual-SIM edge case

Dual-SIM phones have two IMEIs. We prompt the surveyor to select the primary slot — something we missed entirely in V1.

03

Apple MRI installation Surveyor

Apple MRI doesn't live on the device. It's installed each time via a provisioning profile — a 6-step process involving Settings, Safari, and a trust prompt. Surveyors were getting stuck at step 3 or 4 with no guidance and no one to call.

We built a screen-by-screen guide inside the Flipkart app that mirrors what's happening on the device being tested. Progress indicator, estimated time remaining. The surveyor always knows where they are.

Biggest support ticket, fixed

iOS version incompatibility was the #1 reason diagnostics failed. We now check the device's iOS version before starting — and surface a clear, actionable message if it's unsupported, rather than letting the install silently fail three steps in.

04

Reading the results Both

Apple MRI returns a detailed technical report — battery cycle count, sensor pass/fail states, component health percentages. Valuable data. Completely unreadable to the average seller.

We mapped every MRI output to Flipkart's existing condition taxonomy: Like New / Good / Fair / Poor. Sellers see a plain-language summary alongside the raw data. Each failing component gets a brief explanation in English, not tech speak.

The unexpected winner

We added a one-tap "Share to WhatsApp" export almost as an afterthought. It became the most-used feature on the results screen. Sellers were sending the report to buyers before the device even shipped.

05

Yaantra Android / Fallback

Apple MRI only runs on Apple devices. A significant chunk of Flipkart's refurbished listings are Android. We integrated Yaantra — a third-party diagnostic tool — using the same guided install flow, the same results mapping, and the same report format as Apple MRI. Sellers see an identical experience regardless of platform. The backend is different. The seller doesn't need to know that.

06

Getting sellers to actually opt in Discovery

The diagnostic was optional. When it was buried at step 7 of the listing flow, barely anyone opted in. The feature existed but it was invisible. We ran an A/B test across three entry point variants: inline prompt mid-flow, full interstitial, and a bottom sheet at the very start of listing creation.

+31%

opt-in lift — bottom sheet at listing start vs. mid-flow inline prompt

Framing mattered too. "Add a Verified badge to your listing" outperformed "Run a device diagnostic" by a significant margin. Same action, completely different story.
